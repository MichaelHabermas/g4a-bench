Each cell carries reduction · trust rung (art=artifact, md=reported-math) · a judgment chip (✓ meaningful, ? needs judgment, ✗ superficial/contested).

A · Progressive disclosure B · Flat matrix C · Per-repo scorecards
Option A

Progressive disclosure — whole → parts → evidence

Scales to a full week. You start at category rollups (the whole), expand one category into its sub-metric matrix (the parts), then click a cell for evidence + judgment. Costs a click to see detail. Rollup never averages — it shows the spread.

Option B

Flat matrix — every sub-metric is a column

Everything visible at once, no clicks. Great for spotting one anomaly across the whole grid. Drowns at week scale — 8 categories × ~5 sub-metrics = 30-40 columns, horizontal scroll, and no whole/part structure. This is closest to the dump you didn't like.

Option C

Per-repo scorecards — one card per repo

Reads like a report card — great for understanding one repo deeply, or handing a single team their results. Cross-repo comparison is weaker: to compare dalton's as work to shiv's you flip between cards instead of reading across a row.
